<div id='comment z12jvxra4uj3xpt2j22yjhfo3pvvehdgr04'>

        <p>My favorite system to read and sigh about dreamily is in Freebooters on the Frontier, but I highly doubt I could get me players to play with it. It basically has you randomly roll to generate D&amp;D-ish spell names, and then spend spell points every time you cast to flexibly tailor the effects. Sounds like great fun to me, but way too fiddly for my group. <br /><br />My favorite fictional &quot;system&quot; for (or explanation of) magic is kind of hinted at in the Amber books. Not just the way immortal demigods can alter the world at will (but that is cool too), but the way Merlin describes &quot;hanging&quot; a spell, if I remember the term right—like he did the ritual prep and now just has to trigger it, like a mousetrap—always made way more sense to me than &quot;memorizing.&quot;<br /><br />But I bet some other folks will reply and make me go &quot;oh, I wish I&#39;d remembered that one. I do rather like that one.&quot;</p>
</div>
