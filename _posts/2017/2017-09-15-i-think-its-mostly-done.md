        <p>Yeah, I know in Warhammer, there&#39;s both 2-weapons wardancer and 2 handed weapons wardancers.<br /><br />I didn&#39;t want to give the Wardancer access to +1 dmg weapons (at least not before a lvl 6-10 move) because in my mind, this class is about fighting a lot of enemies at once. Felt like +1 dmg was a lot when you can hit 2-3 enemies at once.<br /><br />I can definately change to 2 short blades though. I felt it was more thematic if they had access to special weapons (not available in the DW book).<br /><br />Allowing choice between 2 different wardance style moves at character creation is a good idea. I&#39;ll have to think about an alternate move though. It also mean that the discarded choice can never be taken.<br /><br />To me, wardancer is elf only. Mecanically, it doesn&#39;t really matter, but thematically, it&#39;s something deeply attached to the wood elf culture. I see it as a rare art that is deeply safeguarded. No wardancer master would ever want to show this to a non-wood-elf.</p>
</div>

Perhaps this could be built into some moves, or maybe this is just a distraction.<br /><br />I like the idea of being able to spend flow after the battle ends. E.g. through meditation or something, where you could spend flow 1:1 to do things like heal 1d4 or maybe give a +1 to Discern Realities. This itself could be an advanced move, or a series of moves.<br /><br />Just thinking out loud.</p>
