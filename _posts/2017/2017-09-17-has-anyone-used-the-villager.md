I just started a separate thread to discuss playtesting Stonetop.  If anyone else is interested, let me know!<br /><br /><span class="proflinkWrapper"><span class="proflinkPrefix">+</span><a class="proflink" href="https://plus.google.com/100309716718451042779" oid="100309716718451042779">Charlie Etheridge-Nunn</a></span> I haven&#39;t used the Villager playbook, but I&#39;m familiar with it.  The biggest issue that I foresee with using it alongside Class Warfare is that the Class Warfare specialties all have very specific fictional requirements. When you start off making level 1 characters with Class Warfare, it&#39;s assumed that you&#39;ve already met those fictional requirements (or close enough).  But when you start off as a (more-or-less) 0-level Villager, those fictional requirements are almost certainly going to be either not hit or (more likely) ignored.  <br /><br />Which, I guess, brings up the issues that I have with the Villager playbook in general.  It&#39;s a clever idea, but the &quot;0-to-hero&quot; idea works a lot better when there&#39;s a less drastic jump between 0 and 1st level (see Funnel World &amp; Freebooters on the Frontier, or Dungeon Crawl Classics).  In a game like DW, where the fighter is The Fighter and the wizard is The Wizard, that jump from local schlub is a lot bigger and more noticeable.<br /><br />I&#39;m also not really a fan of the Villager&#39;s mechanics. The way you eventually level up is by assigning bonds, and how you assign bonds isn&#39;t very clear (I <i>think</i> it&#39;s supposed to happen when the fiction demands it, but I get this feeling that there&#39;s supposed to be a metagame moment when this happens, like End of Session... and if not, then what&#39;s to stop me from just spamming bonds and wracking up all the advanced villager moves?).  The &quot;write a bond, get a move&quot; thing seems like a very dissociated mechanic to me, and it&#39;s a bit weird that I might gain some of these abilities and then lose them when I level up.  <br /><br />Plus, for your purposes... the Villager playbook &quot;ends&quot; with the PCs leveling up and (according to the Life of Adventure move) leaving town. But it sounds like you&#39;re more interested in keeping things local?  If so, I think that last move will grind a little against your goals.</p>
