If you (like me) find clocks to be an essential part of running any game, you might like these clocks I made for running Dungeon World on Roll20. They follow the Blades in the Dark approach of 4, 6, 8 and 12 segment clocks.<br /><br />Get the clocks here: <a href="https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1otgGi1cCqqfdtU_0ubUHpYIWtWLonhaW" class="ot-anchor">https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1otgGi1cCqqfdtU_0ubUHpYIWtWLonhaW</a><br /><br />You&#39;ll want to set them up as a rollable token like so: <a href="https://wiki.roll20.net/Collections#Creating_a_Rollable_Table_Token" class="ot-anchor">https://wiki.roll20.net/Collections#Creating_a_Rollable_Table_Token</a> (this will let you adjust the clock on the fly in play). <br /><br />If you&#39;re not familiar with clocks, this Medium post is a good overview: <a href="https://medium.com/@kwhitaker81/locked-clocked-and-ready-to-rock-c42ac20ffbd5" class="ot-anchor">https://medium.com/@kwhitaker81/locked-clocked-and-ready-to-rock-c42ac20ffbd5</a> <br /><br />Do post a screenshot if you use them, I love seeing how people set up their games in Roll20.
