      <p><cite>2018-07-22 13:12:38 (edited: 2018-07-22 13:12:38)</cite></p>
        <p>As I always seem to have Barbarians in my teams I usually make enemies loose limbs and other extremities. If they&#39;re not magical beings that usually means one hit is enough for them to bleed out/run away/stand staring at their guts spilling on the floor. It also means that my barbarians have to Defy Danger if they don&#39;t wish to mutilate their combatants, and I use that as complications a lot, while still making them awesome death-machines.</p>
</div>
        

<div id='comment z133xhvh2wykz3umo23njdfqcxerin4kk'>
  <h4><img src='{{site.baseurl}}//images/avatars/102595580176380683252_photo.jpg'> Jeremy Strandberg</h4>
      <p><cite>2018-07-22 14:40:55 (edited: 2018-07-22 14:40:55)</cite></p>
        <p>After the PCs have taken <i>messy</i> damage, I&#39;ve required them to Defy Danger in order to stop the bleeding, on a 7-9 requiring the use of bandages without regaining any HP.  <br /><br />Or I&#39;ve just <b>told them the requirements and asked</b>: &quot;yeah, your pet tiger has a bunch of really deep, nasty puncture wounds from where the rage drake bit her; you&#39;re going to have to expend 2 uses of bandages just to stabilize the wounds, <i>then</i> we talk about regaining HP.&quot;<br /><br />When fighting a large mole-man monster with a buzz-saw arm, the Fighter got a 7-9 to H&amp;S and took like 6 or 7 damage.  Not enough for me to feel like dismembering him, but I used its monster move of &quot;shred metal, stone, flesh, etc.&quot; to say that his shield was destroyed and it bit into his arm and down to the bone.  I don&#39;t recall if I made him Defy Danger to do anything but scream in pain, but I should have.  I know I gave the Weakened debility.  (They used a healing potion to heal his flesh &amp; bone and clear the debility, instead of HP.)<br /><br />Against a gwythaint (giant eagle/vulture/pteradactyl thing) that did <i>messy</i> damage, again on a 7-9, I had it yank the rogue&#39;s arm out of his socket.  <br /><br />Against an assassin vine (which does <i>messy</i> damage), I took it to mean that the damage was from deep, nasty puncture wounds from the thorns. It shredded their pants/boots and they had to bandage the hell out of themselves <i>and</i> go back to town for new clothes.  <br /><br />I&#39;ve written up monsters, like a treant sort of thing, with a quality that they were immune to weapons that weren&#39;t <i>forceful</i> and <i>messy</i>.  Not sure I think that&#39;s a good idea, though.  <br /><br />Plus all sorts of stuff like people described above:  enemies losing limbs (even though they still have HP), spectacularly nasty &amp; gory wounds, etc. <br /></p>
</div>
