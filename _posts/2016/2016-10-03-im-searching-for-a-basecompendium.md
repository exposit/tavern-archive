I&#39;m searching for a base/compendium class that lets me play a wizard in a more &quot;Gandalf&quot; Fashion. The core wizard is not the feeling i&#39;m searching for; I think that maybe I should mix the druid and the bard, but I&#39;m sure that somebody must have done a hack already.<br />Bonus question: Is there any playbook to play an Earthsea wizard?
<div id='comment z124idpwosq0hb1uq23si5lq0oa2tjid5'>
  <h4><img src='{{site.baseurl}}//images/avatars/104561179674739270437_photo.jpg'> Dan Bryant</h4>
      <p><cite>2016-10-03 14:43:03 (edited: 2016-10-03 14:45:54)</cite></p>
        <p>There is a Mage class in the Dungeon World Secrets and Sorcery pack by Peter Johansen.  It&#39;s a much more flexible caster archetype, with the caveat that their magic can be a bit unpredictable.  You can push it in a lot of different directions, but you should get get buy-in with your GM, as it can be a tricky class to manage, due to the open-ended nature of the their magic and the side effects that need to be worked in.  It&#39;s the type of class that works best with an experienced player who is willing to help balance their power with shortcomings.  Still, if you and the GM can work together well on fleshing out the concept you&#39;re after, it can create a pretty cool character.</p>
</div>
