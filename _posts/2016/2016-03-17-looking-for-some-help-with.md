Looking for some help with <span class="proflinkWrapper"><span class="proflinkPrefix">+</span><a class="proflink" href="https://plus.google.com/104915224203075819082" oid="104915224203075819082">Rob Donoghue</a></span>&#39;s notion of flags to replace bonds (<a href="http://walkingmind.evilhat.com/2015/09/07/from-bonds-to-flags/" class="ot-anchor">http://walkingmind.evilhat.com/2015/09/07/from-bonds-to-flags/</a>). Creating these is harder than it sounds, as the idea is to have a player trigger an interesting choice in another player.<br /><br />Here is a list I&#39;ve come up with (including the suggestions from Rob, slightly modified). <b>I&#39;m interested in both feedback on and additions to this list.</b> Bear in mind that flags take the form of instructions to other players and work best when they give you a choice to make. Often this is a choice between an ingrained personality/world view with a current situation. <br /><br />- Accommodating: counter my proposal with a less attractive one I must accept to maintain harmony.<br />- Aspiring: make me an offer that threatens my social standing.<br />- Compassionate: offer me an easier solution that requires I exploit those weaker than me.<br />- Curious: convince me to try something I probably shouldn’t.<br />- Deceitful: believe and act on a lie I’ve told you.<br />- Devoted: offer me an easier solution that requires I compromise my relation with <i>_____________</i>. (Choices include: family or a family member, a particular organization, another party member, a lover, a friend, someone to whom you have sworn allegiance, etc.)<br />- Graceless: include me in a beneficial social interaction I must spoil with blunt observation or crass behavior.<br />- Greedy: offer me financial reward to undermine a friend.<br />- Gullible: tell me a lie I believe.<br />- Heroic: let me keep you from going first into danger so I can go myself<br />- Honest: involve me in a deception I must ruin.<br />- Irresponsible: convince me to shirk my duty.<br />- Peculiar: refuse my aid because I’m different.<br />- Righteous: offer me an easier solution that requires I violate my principle of <i>_____________</i>. (Choices include: ‘non-violence’, various religious beliefs, moral code, sworn vows, ‘chastity’, ‘might makes right’, entitlement, institutional prejudice, etc.)<br />- Sycophantic: insist I publicly compare the greatness of two people from whom I seek favor.<br />- Unsophisticated: exemplify a social convention or intricate concept I must misunderstand.<br />- Visionary: offer me an easier solution that interferes with my dream of <i>_____________</i>.<br /><br />Some others that seem like they might work, but I couldn&#39;t think of a decent conflict/choice they would generate:<br /><br />- Decisive: Rob suggests &quot;allow me to make a decision so you can criticize it&quot;, but this isn&#39;t really a choice or story conflict (other than player bickering).<br />- Gracious<br />- Hedonistic<br />- Humble<br />- Jovial/jockular (ruin the moment with humor)<br />- Judgemental<br />- Rebellious<br />- Trustworthy<br />- Vain

        <p>I was just putting the finishing touches on my own replacement for Bonds when I saw this post. I had looked at flags and liked the general idea, but also found that creating new flags was <b>hard</b>. Kudos for doing the hard work.<br /><br />What I have the hardest time with is the trigger conditions and the agency of the choice. E.g., these two flags<br />Gullible: tell me a lie I believe.<br />Deceitful: believe and act on a lie I’ve told you.<br />Are essentially the same just with the trigger and choice agency swapped between character with the flag and the other PCs. I think this could end up being very confusing.</p>

  <h4><img src='{{site.baseurl}}//images/avatars/115471242540460576883_photo.jpg'> Zachary Cross</h4>
      <p><cite>2016-03-18 17:00:58 (edited: 2016-03-18 17:00:58)</cite></p>
        <p>Ultimately, a character&#39;s flags are a request to &quot;enable me to be [FLAG]&quot;.  This may involve another character setting up a situation or it may involve another character following through on a situation, but at the end of the session, if the players can say &quot;Thank you for enabling me to be [FLAG]&quot;, then they&#39;ve hit the flag / earned the XP.<br /><br />So, the Guillible vs. Deceitful flags are basically mirrors of each other, and that&#39;s ok!  You could probably rephrase deceitful to put the responsibility for setup back on the other character with something like &quot;Invite my input and don&#39;t question it (at least for the moment)&quot; if you wanted.<br /><br /></p>
</div>

        <p>I think it&#39;s important to remember that flags should ultimately enable taking action in a way meaningful to your character, and that they bring enough consequence to the story to develop a relationship between the enabler and the enablee. So it&#39;s not just a matter of making them profound, they also need to tie into the playbook and create momentum.</p>
